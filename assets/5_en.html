1. Calculating the Approximate Delivery Date and Estimated Fetal Weight

    2. Approximate Delivery Date will use Naegele formula

    Naegele formula: the first day of the last menstrual + 7-3 months = date of birth

    Naegel formula can be used only if the mother menstruate regularly. This formula can not be used if:

    1. Mother has a history of irregular menstruation and menstrual (amenorhoe)

    2. Mother was pregnant while still breastfeeding and have never menstruate again after giving birth

    3. Pregnant women after stop consuming the pill and not menstruating again If one of the above situations occur, the approximate date of delivery made clinically (example: by looking at the size of the uterus) or by using ultrasound.

    Example Usage Naegele formula:

    Starting from the first day of the last menstrual period and add 7 days. Then count backward three months Note well, use your fingers or hands and write a paper to make it easier.

    LMP = Last Menstruation Period

    LMP: May 5

    Add 7 days: May 6 + 7 days = May 13

    Counting back three months: 13 April, March 13, February 13

    Approximate Delivery Date are on February 13th in the next year.

    LMP: 30 November

    Add 7 days: 30 November + 7 days = December 7

    Counting back three months: Nov. 7, 7 October, 7 september

    Approximate Delivery Date are on 7th September in the next year.

    Estimated Fetal Weight (EFW)

    When the presenting part not yet entered pelvis

    (EFW)= (HUF-12)x155

    When the presenting part has entered the pelvis

    (EFW)= (HUF-11)x155

    Information:

    EFW: Estimated Fetal Weight (g)

    HUF: High Uterine Fundus (cm) measured by metheline

    Example: HUF: 30 cm and the fetal head has not entered pelvis

    EFW= (30-12) x155 = 2790 grams

  2. Definition of Childbirth Preparation

    3. a. Childbirth is a plan of action developed by the mother, family members, and midwives.

    b. This plan does not have to be in writing and usually is not written. This plan is just a discussion to ensure that women receive the care she needed.

    c. With the birth plan would reduce confusion and chaos at the time of delivery and increases the likelihood that the mother will receive appropriate care and timely.

  3. 5 essential components birth plan

    4. Matters concerning the making birth plan is as follows.

    1. Creating a birth plan

      1. Place of delivery.

        1. Selection of place of delivery is determined by the value of the risk of pregnancy and childbirth types are planned. Low-risk deliveries can be done in the clinic, polindes, or maternity homes, while high-risk deliveries to be done in a hospital that has an operating room facilities, blood transfusion, and treatment of high-risk infants.

        2. Maternity recommended implemented in public hospitals / hospital mother and child, complete with trained personnel and adequate equipment. Due to transportation and health personnel is still limited in some areas most of the labor was assisted by TBAs and take place at home. The condition is an obstacle that is difficult to overcome until today.

        3. In other countries (eg in the United States and the Netherlands), deliveries can be done at home because it has advantages compared to hospital births. Home atmosphere make the mother more comfortable so the delivery process more smoothly and the role of the husband's apparent perceived. Nevertheless, home deliveries require infrastructure support, as well as relief workers readiness to face any eventualities that occur during delivery and postpartum.

      2. Choose a trained health provider

        1. Health personnel attending births is allowed general practitioners, midwives, and doctors in obstetrics and gynecology.

        2. Selection of auxiliary power delivery is determined by the patient, the value of the risk of pregnancy, and the type of delivery that will be planned for each patient.

        3. Selection of patients based on risk is intended to handle cases more directed and handled by competent personnel. At the time of delivery, the handling of the case more carefully performed by taking into account the characteristics of cases. Should all have the case considered high risk because there is no way any one can foresee that the labor must walk a normal so that any birth attendant will always be cautious and prepare everything to overcome the complications that may occur.

        4. Moreover, economic, religious, social, cultural and sometimes also influence the choice of birth attendants

    2. Make a plan for decision-making in case of emergency when no key decision makers.

      1. Who is the ultimate decision maker in the family?

      2. Who will make the decision if the primary decision makers when there is no emergency?

    3. Preparing transport system in case of emergencies

      1. Many mothers who died suffered serious complications during pregnancy, delivery or postpartum and does not have the range of transportation that can take them to the level of health care that can provide care that is competent to deal with them

      2. Each family should have a transportation plan for the mother if she had complications and needs to be referred to a higher level of care.

      This plan needs to be prepared early in pregnancy, and must consist of elements below

      1. Where will the mother maternity (villages, health facilities, hospitals).

      2. How do I reach the level of care that is more in case of emergency).

      3. All health facilities where mothers will be referred.

      4. How do I get funding in case of emergency.

      5. How do I find a blood donor

    4. Make a Financial plan / saving patterns.

      1. Families are encouraged to save some money for supplies funds to care during pregnancy and in case of emergencies.

      2. Saving and programmed according to their ability.

      3. Collective agreements in the family.

      4. Saving Mother Maternity Program

    5. Preparing the equipment needed for delivering baby

      1. Mother and family can gather belongings (such as sanitary napkins, soap, maternity clothes, baby clothes, etc.) and save them for childbirth.

      2. Some hospitals have already made a list of equipment that should be taken when it comes, for example octopus, toiletries (soap, toothbrush, toothpaste, shampoo, deodorant, powder, combs, lip balm, small towels, big towels), personal equipment (tampons, pads bra, bras for breastfeeding, panties, some blouses, sandals, t-shirts loose / negligee and socks), cream nipples, sponge small, washlap, fabric, baby clothes, and diapers.

      3. It should be prepared in advance, put in a bag so that signs of labor appeared, the mother did not panic and can immediately seek help (to hospitals, maternity homes, and so forth).

      4. Preparing for baby gear can be a flurry of fun.
