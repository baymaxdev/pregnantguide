1. Nutritional Needs Every Trimester of Pregnancy

    2. The first trimester (gestational age 1-3 months)
    1. It is a preparation period mother to her pregnancy
    2. The growth of the fetus is still going slow so the nutritional needs of the fetus is not a lot of growth
    3. The nutritional needs of pregnant women in this period are the same as regular adult women
    4. Given that complaints arise at first trimester is a lack of appetite, nausea, dizziness, hallucinations, want to eat weird, nausea, vomiting and others.
    5. Within certain limits it is still reasonable, necessary it is recommended is to eat foods that are easily digested form in small portions but often
    6. 6Material given good food is dried and fresh food such as toast, biscuits and cereals as well as fresh fruit or juice
    7. Complaints emesis (vomiting) can be avoided by not eating and drinking together or should be spaced about 15-30 minutes.
    Second trimester (4-6 months) and third (7-9 months)
    1. The rapid growth of the fetus takes place during this time
    2. 50% of BB additions occurred in the sixth and seventh
    3. Increased appetite
    4. The ability to digest food is getting better
    5. At this time additional sugars needed to maintain good health
