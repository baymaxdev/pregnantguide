Every month, the ovarium of women release one to two ovum (ovulation) that is captured by the fimbrae and the womb. When sexual intercourse time, semen spilled into the vagina and millions of sperm cells move into the uterine cavity and into the ovum. Fertilization of an ovum by a sperm is common in an expanding part of the fallopian tuve (ámpula tuba).

In many gathered around the ovum sperm issued yeast to dilute substances that protect the ovum then the easiest place to enter, sign of the sperm and then unite with the ovum. This event is called fertilization (conception). The fertilized ovum is immediately splitting hairs vibrate while moving the tube towards the womb space is then attached to the mucosa of the uterus for the next gloved in the womb. This event is called nidasi (attachment).

From conception to nidasi takes approximately six to seven days. Supplied blood and nutrients to the embryo and fetus, and prepared uri (placenta). Thus, it can be said that for each pregnancy there should be an ovum (egg), spermatozoa (sperm), fertilization (conception), nidasi, and placenta.
